Model Overview

Our Fake News Detector uses a Random Forest Classifier trained on a diverse dataset of news articles. The model analyzes various linguistic and structural features to determine the likelihood of an article containing misleading information.

How Our Random Forest Model Works

Our fake news detection system uses a Random Forest machine learning model. Here's how it works:

Random Forest Explained:
  1. Multiple Decision Trees: Random Forest builds many decision trees that each analyze features like readability, sentiment, and lexical diversity.
  2. Majority Voting: Each tree votes on whether an article is real or fake news.
  3. Confidence Score: For real news, the confidence shows how confident the model is about it being real. For fake news, the confidence shows how confident the model is about it being fake.
  4. Feature Importance: Our model found readability score to be the most important feature, followed by sentiment and lexical diversity.

This approach provides better accuracy and stability than single decision trees or simpler models.

Key Features Used:

Readability Score

Measures how easy or difficult the text is to read using the Flesch Reading Ease score (0-100).

VADER Sentiment

Analyzes emotional tone using VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner) with scores from -1 to +1.

Lexical Diversity

Measures vocabulary richness by calculating the ratio of unique words to total words (0-1).

Feature Details

Readability Score (Flesch Reading Ease)

Measures how easy it is to read the article text. Higher scores indicate easier reading, lower scores indicate more complex text.

Range Interpretation Description Why It Matters
90-100 Very easy 5th grade level Very high readability typical of children's content or simplistic writing
60-70 Standard 8-9th grade level Balanced readability common in mainstream publications
30-50 Difficult College level More complex content found in academic or specialized publications
<30 Very difficult Academic or obfuscated Extremely complex language can be a sign of intentional obfuscation

Model Impact: Articles with very low readability scores (<40) tend to follow the fake news path in our model, as fake news often uses emotionally charged, exaggerated, or complex phrasing to manipulate or confuse readers.

VADER Sentiment

Analyzes the emotional tone of the article using a specialized sentiment analysis tool calibrated for social media content.

Range Interpretation Description Examples
-1 to -0.5 Extremely negative Strong negative emotions Words like "terrible", "horrific", "disaster", "outrageous"
-0.5 to -0.05 Moderately negative Mild negative tone Words like "problem", "concerning", "difficult", "unfortunate"
-0.05 to +0.05 Neutral Balanced tone Factual reporting, minimal emotional language
+0.05 to +0.5 Moderately positive Mild positive tone Words like "good", "beneficial", "promising", "successful"
+0.5 to +1 Extremely positive Strong positive emotions Words like "excellent", "amazing", "breakthrough", "revolutionary"

Model Impact: Fake news typically uses extremely positive or negative language to sway opinion, while legitimate journalism tends toward more neutral, balanced reporting. Very extreme sentiment scores (above 0.8 or below -0.8) often indicate manipulation.

Lexical Diversity

Measures the ratio of unique words to total words in the text, indicating vocabulary richness and writing quality.

Range Interpretation Description Content Quality
<0.4 Low diversity Repetitive or templated content Same phrases repeated, limited vocabulary, may indicate template-based or auto-generated content
0.4-0.6 Medium diversity Standard writing Normal variation of vocabulary, typical of standard news articles
>0.6 High diversity Well-written, varied content Rich vocabulary, careful word choice, fewer repetitions, typical of high-quality journalism

Model Impact: Fake articles often repeat phrases and use formulaic language patterns. High-quality journalism typically employs varied vocabulary to cover nuance. Our model often flags articles with lexical diversity below 0.4 as suspicious.

Limitations and Future Work

Current Limitations:
  • The model may not catch sophisticated fake news that mimics legitimate news writing styles
  • Performance may vary with different languages and cultural contexts
  • Satire and opinion pieces might be incorrectly flagged
Future Improvements:
  • Integration of fact-checking APIs
  • Addition of image analysis capabilities
  • Support for multiple languages
  • Improved handling of satire and opinion content
  • Incorporation of user feedback to enhance model accuracy over time
