Fake News Detector

Model Overview

Our Fake News Detector uses a Random Forest Classifier trained on a diverse dataset of news articles. The model analyzes various linguistic and structural features to determine the likelihood of an article containing misleading information.

Key Features Used:

Sentiment Analysis

Analyzes the overall emotional tone of the article using TextBlob's sentiment analysis.

Readability Score

Measures how easy or difficult the text is to read using the Flesch Reading Ease score.

Word Count

Analyzes the length of the article to identify potential information density issues.

VADER Sentiment

Uses VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner) for more nuanced sentiment analysis.

Why These Features?

Our feature selection is based on extensive research in fake news detection and natural language processing. Here's why each feature is important:

  • Sentiment Analysis:

    Fake news often uses emotionally charged language to manipulate readers. By analyzing sentiment, we can identify potential bias and emotional manipulation.

  • Readability Score:

    Misleading articles sometimes use complex language to obscure facts or confuse readers. The readability score helps identify such patterns.

  • Word Count:

    Article length can indicate the depth of coverage. Extremely short or long articles might be red flags for potential misinformation.

  • VADER Sentiment:

    VADER is specifically attuned to social media and web content, making it particularly effective for analyzing modern news articles.

Limitations and Future Work

Current Limitations:

  • The model may not catch sophisticated fake news that mimics legitimate news writing styles
  • Performance may vary with different languages and cultural contexts
  • Satire and opinion pieces might be incorrectly flagged

Future Improvements:

  • Integration of fact-checking APIs
  • Addition of image analysis capabilities
  • Support for multiple languages
  • Improved handling of satire and opinion content
